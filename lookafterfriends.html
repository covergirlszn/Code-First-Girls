Look After Friends

Being there for your friend can be essential for a persons recovery. The friendship could mean a lot to a person who is dealing with mental health ilness such as depression. People who experience depression often believe they are worthless and that no one cares about their existence. However, you can change these feelings by being listening to their issues and showing compassion or by just simply being present.



Sometimes it can be hard to figure out how to help another in distress and that is OK. However, we are here to help you and provide you tips on how you can help your friend. Below are some suggestions which you can try.
  • Listen to what your friend has to say and do not judge them.
  • Encourage your friend to engage in social activities such as going for lunch
  • Call them on a regular basis, just to let them know you are there for them!
  • Encourage your friend to take on a new hobbie such as dancing or iceskating
  • Find local self-help groups that your friend can attend.
  • Make sure you pay attention to them when they are showing signs of self-harm and let their family know. In case of emergency, call the 999.
